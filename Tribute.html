Queen Elizabeth II

The longest-reigning female monarch in world history

Timeline of Queen Elizabeth II's life:

  • 1926 - Born in Mayfair, London
  • 1930 - Early years were spent between Piccadilly in London and at White Lodge in Richmond Park
  • 1933 - Her Majesty was seven years old, her father King George VI brought home Dookie, a Pembrokeshire Welsh corgi.
  • 1936 - Elizabeth’s father becomes king when his older brother, King Edward VIII steps down.
  • 1942 - Elizabeth made her first public engagement on her 16th birthday by inspecting the soldiers of the Grenadier Guards
  • 1947 - Elizabeth, aged 21 at the time, married Philip Mountbatten in Westminster Abbey.
  • 1948 - Elizabeth gave birth to her first child, Prince Charles, at Buckingham Palace.
  • 1950 - Princess Anne was born in Clarence House.
  • 1953 - Following the death of her father the previous year, Elizabeth’s coronation took place in Westminster Abbey at 27.
  • 1960 - Prince Andrew was born at Buckingham Palace, her third child.
  • 1964 - At the age of 37, Queen Elizabeth gave birth to her fourth and final child, Prince Edward, her fourth child.
  • 1977 - The Queen's silver jubilee
  • 1982 - Prince William was born to Prince Charles and Diana, Princess of Wales
  • 1997 - The Queen’s daughter-in-law, Diana, Princess of Wales, died on August 31.
  • 2002 - The Queen's golden jubilee
  • 2007 - Queen Elizabeth II becomes the oldest-ever reigning British monarch
  • 2015 - The Queen became the longest-reigning monarch in British history (more than 63 years)
  • 2016 - Queen Elizabeth celebrated her 90th birthday
  • 2020 - Queen Elizabeth delivered a special broadcast in relation to the coronavirus outbreak
  • 2021 - The Queen’s loving husband of 73 years, Prince Philip, the Duke of Edinburgh, died aged 99
  • 2022 - The Queen's platinum jubilee.
  • 2022 - The Queen leave Buckingham Palace and made a permanent move to Windsor Castle
  • 2022 - Queen Elizabeth II dies aged 96
